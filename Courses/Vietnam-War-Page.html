Vietnam War Overview

The Origin of the Vietnam War

  • The war started on November 1st, 1955 and ended on April 30th, 1975
  • Vietnam wanted independence after French colonialism. After WWII, Vietnam was divided into Communist North vs Anti-Communist South
  • The US supported the South while the USSR and China supported the North, leading to the Domino Theory concerns

The Key Events that took place during the war

  1. Geneva Accords divided Vietnam at the 17th parallel (1954)
  2. The Gulf of Tonkin incident (1964), US sent combat troops
  3. The Tet Offensive, large North Vietnamese attack (1968)
  4. The My Lai Massacre, mass murder of civilians in South Vietnam (1968)
  5. Vietnamization (1969â€“1973), US began withdrawing troops
  6. Signing of the Paris Peace Accords (1973), ending the war and restoring peace
  7. The fall of Saigon (1975), North Vietnam victory and reunification

The Aftermath of the Vietnam War

Millions of Vietnamese civilians and soldiers died, including approximately 58,000 American service members. The US military was demoralized, and the nation was divided. Vietnam was reunified under communist leadership.
