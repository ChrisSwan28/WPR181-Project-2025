Key Events that Took Place During World War 2

  1. Germany's invasion of Poland (1939)
  2. Germany invaded Denmark, Norway, Belgium, Netherlands, and France (1940)
  3. Germany's invasion of the Soviet Union (Operation Barbarossa) (1941)
  4. The Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor; Germany and Italy declared war on the USA (1941)
  5. The Holocaust policy was formalised, known as The Final Solution (1942)
  6. The Battle of Midway (1942)
  7. The Battle of Stalingrad (1942)
  8. Germany surrendered at Stalingrad (1943)
  9. D-Day landings in Normandy (1944)
  10. Germany surrendered; Adolf Hitler committed suicide; USA dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki (1945)

The Origin of World War 2

  • The full timeline was from September 1, 1939 to June 22, 1945
  • The Treaty of Versailles imposed harsh penalties on Germany after WW1
  • The Great Depression helped the Nazi Party gain support
  • The League of Nations was too weak to maintain peace, allowing Hitler to expand
  • The Nazi-Soviet Pact allowed Germany to invade Poland without fear of Soviet interference

The Aftermath of World War 2

The war caused over 60 million deaths and left cities and economies in ruin. Germany was divided and Japan became a democracy. The United Nations was created to promote peace and decolonization of empires, which led to the beginning of the Cold War. Much of Asia and Europe was destroyed, prompting large reconstruction efforts.
